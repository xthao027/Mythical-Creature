This page will cover more animal characteritic creatures, such as those that have little to no human limbs and live on dry land. Of course, this will only cover a few, the internet has other websites that cover more (look on the 'Extra' page for reference).

Some believe the word ogres orginates near the European region, maybe France, Hungary, or even Italy. Whatever the case, Ogres have shown up in countless myths and fairtales. Identified with their hideous expression and rough body. Their skin varies from blue to green, they are dirty as well as unintelligent. They are found quite far from human civilizations, maybe deeper off in the woods or in a cave. But of course, not every ogre is bad and stupid, think of Shrek (2001).

Goblins and dwarves are quite similar to orges. And giants might be even closer. Goblins are dipicted as smaller than an ogre with visibly green skin. Goblins are more often than not affiliated with treasures, such as gold and jeweleries. In many games, they are depicted as greedy, hoarding and looting treasure. Goblins vary drastically among different regions and religions. Similar to ogres, giants are big (as their name suggests) and smelly. They, as well as ogres, are sometimes depicted as human-eaters. Giants are depicted with huge strength and aggressive, as well as bad-tempered. One popular children's story is Jack and the Beanstalk, where the main character encounters an agressive and unintelligent giant. Lastly, there are dwarfs. Dwarfs are small creatures that are affiliated with wisdom and craftsmanship. Another children's story is Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs, showcasing the small seven dwarfs that work hard in the mines.

Greek

Minotaurs, as depicted by Greek Mythology, is a half-man and half-bull. The head is clearly a bull with two horns and he has a tail, but his body of a muscular man. The orgin behind the Minotaur, and, like every Greek myth, is messed up with different interpretations. The Minotaur is the offspring between Pasiphae, Minos' wife, and a bull sent by Poseiden for sacrficial reasonings to Minos. Minos kept the bull alive and Poseidon punished his wife by making her fall in love with it. The child, the Minotaur, was locked up in a labyrinth by Minos.

In Greek Myth, nymphs smaller female deities responsible for a specific natural place. They come in all form and have different temperments. All are depicted as beautiful maidens. Within the name of "nymph," there lies subcategories and names of nymphs that made a bigger impact. A few different categories under nymphs are Naiads (nymphs that watches over fresh bodies of waters--such as fountains and streams), Hyades (nymphs that bring about rain), and Meliae (nymphs of ash trees).
